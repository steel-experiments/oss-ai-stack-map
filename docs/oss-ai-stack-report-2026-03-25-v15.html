+ main findings

The modern OSS AI stack is provider-first, orchestration-heavy, and surprisingly multi-vendor.

[finding]

Providers sit at the center

Provider SDKs account for 31.2% of all normalized edges, and OpenAI appears in 550 final repos. The provider layer is the most common and the most connective part of the stack.

[finding]

Multi-provider stacks are common

The strongest repeated pairing is Anthropic SDK plus OpenAI SDK in 251 repos, followed by OpenAI plus Google GenAI in 209. Major OSS projects are not clustering around a single vendor.

[finding]

The ecosystem is backend-heavy

Training, orchestration, providers, and retrieval dominate the graph. Evaluation and observability remain comparatively thin, with only 34 guardrail/eval edges and 55 observability edges.

+ stack profile

A compact read on the default stack shape

Inference from the aggregate counts: the modal major OSS AI repo in this snapshot is organization-owned, Python-first, anchored on a provider SDK, often layers in Hugging Face training tools, and then adds orchestration, retrieval, and a lightweight UI shell.

+ coverage and method

What the dataset captures well, and where it still undercounts

[1] Direct evidence dominates

954 final repos have manifests and 824 have SBOM dependency evidence. 55 final repos map only through README fallback, so direct evidence still dominates but does not fully cover the published set.

[2] Normalization still leaves gaps

14 included repos (1.4%) have no normalized technology edge, so graph-like analysis describes the mapped subset, not the entire final population.

[3] Judge usage stays explicit

581 repos were judge-reviewed and 158 judge overrides were applied in this snapshot. The published set remains rule-first, but not purely rule-only.
